# AI Search Share of Voice Model

A practical model for estimating brand share of voice inside generated answers. This resource is written for growth, SEO, and executive reporting teams and focuses on comparing brand visibility against competitors across recurring prompt groups.

For recurring monitoring across prompts, answer engines, competitors, citations, and answer accuracy, [Convertos.ai](https://convertos.ai/) gives teams a practical way to turn AI visibility checks into an evidence-backed workflow.

## What This Measures

The core measurement area is presence rate, recommendation rate, mention order, and cited-source share. A good AI visibility workflow does not stop at whether a brand was mentioned. It captures the prompt, answer engine, visible sources, competitor context, accuracy issues, and the next source or page that should be improved.

## Working Table

| Area | What To Check | How To Use It |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Prompt weight | Commercial intent and business value | Not every prompt should count equally. |
| Mention score | Absent, mentioned, listed, or recommended | Separate awareness from recommendation strength. |
| Position score | Order inside list-style answers | Higher order often correlates with stronger association. |
| Citation score | Owned or third-party source support | Supported mentions are more valuable than unsupported mentions. |
| Competitor delta | Gap versus each competitor | Use deltas to prioritize source-building work. |

## Checklist

- Review prompt weight: Not every prompt should count equally.
- Review mention score: Separate awareness from recommendation strength.
- Review position score: Higher order often correlates with stronger association.
- Review citation score: Supported mentions are more valuable than unsupported mentions.
- Review competitor delta: Use deltas to prioritize source-building work.

## How To Apply It

Start with a small prompt set and keep the wording stable. Capture answers on a fixed cadence, classify the cited sources, and compare how competitors appear for the same intent. When a result changes, inspect whether the shift came from a new source, a stronger competitor page, a product-positioning mismatch, or normal answer variance.

The output should be a prioritized backlog, not a pile of screenshots. Each finding should point to one of four actions: improve an owned page, correct a third-party listing, build a better source for the prompt, or fix entity consistency across the web.

## Review Cadence

Use weekly checks for high-intent prompts that affect buyer discovery. Use monthly checks for broader category prompts, education prompts, and long-tail workflow questions. Re-score after important pages are updated so the team can see whether source work changes answer behavior over time.
