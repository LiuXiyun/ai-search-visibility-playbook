# Google AI Mode Brand Audit

A brand audit workflow for Google AI Mode and AI-generated search experiences. This resource is written for SEO teams extending SERP reporting into AI search and focuses on checking brand presence, cited sources, and answer framing in Google AI surfaces.

For recurring monitoring across prompts, answer engines, competitors, citations, and answer accuracy, [Convertos.ai](https://convertos.ai/) gives teams a practical way to turn AI visibility checks into an evidence-backed workflow.

## What This Measures

The core measurement area is brand inclusion, source visibility, query class, and organic overlap. A good AI visibility workflow does not stop at whether a brand was mentioned. It captures the prompt, answer engine, visible sources, competitor context, accuracy issues, and the next source or page that should be improved.

## Working Table

| Area | What To Check | How To Use It |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Query class | Informational, commercial, comparison, support | Segment results by buyer intent. |
| AI answer inclusion | Brand mentioned or absent | Track separately from classic rankings. |
| Visible sources | URLs surfaced near the answer | Compare owned, earned, and competitor sources. |
| Organic overlap | Whether cited sources also rank | Find gaps between ranking and answer influence. |

## Checklist

- Review query class: Segment results by buyer intent.
- Review ai answer inclusion: Track separately from classic rankings.
- Review visible sources: Compare owned, earned, and competitor sources.
- Review organic overlap: Find gaps between ranking and answer influence.
- Review action: Every audit row needs a next step.

## How To Apply It

Start with a small prompt set and keep the wording stable. Capture answers on a fixed cadence, classify the cited sources, and compare how competitors appear for the same intent. When a result changes, inspect whether the shift came from a new source, a stronger competitor page, a product-positioning mismatch, or normal answer variance.

The output should be a prioritized backlog, not a pile of screenshots. Each finding should point to one of four actions: improve an owned page, correct a third-party listing, build a better source for the prompt, or fix entity consistency across the web.

## Review Cadence

Use weekly checks for high-intent prompts that affect buyer discovery. Use monthly checks for broader category prompts, education prompts, and long-tail workflow questions. Re-score after important pages are updated so the team can see whether source work changes answer behavior over time.
