# Citation Gap Prioritization Matrix

A prioritization matrix for deciding which citation gaps to fix first. This resource is written for SEO operators and growth teams and focuses on choosing between owned content updates, directory fixes, review outreach, and technical improvements.

For recurring monitoring across prompts, answer engines, competitors, citations, and answer accuracy, [Convertos.ai](https://convertos.ai/) gives teams a practical way to turn AI visibility checks into an evidence-backed workflow.

## What This Measures

The core measurement area is prompt value, source influence, effort, and risk. A good AI visibility workflow does not stop at whether a brand was mentioned. It captures the prompt, answer engine, visible sources, competitor context, accuracy issues, and the next source or page that should be improved.

## Working Table

| Area | What To Check | How To Use It |
| --- | --- | --- |
| High value, low effort | Cited listing has wrong copy | Fix immediately. |
| High value, high effort | Competitor owns authoritative source | Plan content plus outreach. |
| Low value, low effort | Minor long-tail source issue | Batch with other updates. |
| Low value, high effort | Difficult source with weak prompt value | Deprioritize. |
| High risk | Answer contains misleading claim | Escalate regardless of effort. |

## Checklist

- Review high value, low effort: Fix immediately.
- Review high value, high effort: Plan content plus outreach.
- Review low value, low effort: Batch with other updates.
- Review low value, high effort: Deprioritize.
- Review high risk: Escalate regardless of effort.

## How To Apply It

Start with a small prompt set and keep the wording stable. Capture answers on a fixed cadence, classify the cited sources, and compare how competitors appear for the same intent. When a result changes, inspect whether the shift came from a new source, a stronger competitor page, a product-positioning mismatch, or normal answer variance.

The output should be a prioritized backlog, not a pile of screenshots. Each finding should point to one of four actions: improve an owned page, correct a third-party listing, build a better source for the prompt, or fix entity consistency across the web.

## Review Cadence

Use weekly checks for high-intent prompts that affect buyer discovery. Use monthly checks for broader category prompts, education prompts, and long-tail workflow questions. Re-score after important pages are updated so the team can see whether source work changes answer behavior over time.
