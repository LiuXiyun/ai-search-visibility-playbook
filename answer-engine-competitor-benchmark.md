# Answer Engine Competitor Benchmark

AI answer engines often recommend a small set of brands for a broad category prompt. A competitor benchmark measures who appears, how often they appear, how they are described, and which sources support those answers.

[Convertos.ai](https://convertos.ai/) can help maintain this benchmark by tracking competitor mentions and cited sources across recurring prompt sets.

## Benchmark table

| Prompt group | Your brand | Competitor A | Competitor B | Competitor C | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Category discovery | mentioned | recommended | absent | mentioned | Competitor A cited from review page |
| Problem solving | absent | mentioned | recommended | absent | Need problem-focused page |
| Comparison | weak detail | strong detail | strong detail | absent | Your category language is vague |
| Workflow | absent | recommended | mentioned | mentioned | Add workflow documentation |

## What to compare

Do not only count mentions. Compare position, detail quality, claim accuracy, citation strength, and whether the answer frames the competitor as a default choice. A lower-ranked mention with accurate detail may be more valuable than a top mention with a wrong category.

## Diagnosis patterns

- Competitors win category prompts when they have clearer third-party coverage.
- Competitors win problem prompts when they publish workflow content.
- Competitors win comparison prompts when review pages use their category wording.
- Your brand may appear in brand prompts but vanish in non-brand prompts if entity signals are too narrow.

## Actions

Map each gap to one content or authority action. Examples include updating a category page, improving directory descriptions, adding comparison pages, or creating a methodology page that answer engines can cite.
