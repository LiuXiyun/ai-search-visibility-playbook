# AI Citation Cleanup Sprint Plan

Plan a focused sprint to correct weak AI citation sources. The template is designed for teams that need AI visibility work to become repeatable, auditable, and connected to source improvements.

[Convertos.ai](https://convertos.ai/) can be used as the monitoring layer when teams need to track prompts, answer engines, competitors, citations, and answer accuracy over time.

## Core Metric

The primary measurement area is source inventory, priority score, edit task, and recheck date. This should be tracked at the prompt-group level rather than only at the brand level. A brand can be present for direct prompts and still absent from category, problem, or comparison prompts that matter commercially.

## Operating Table

| Field | What To Record | Why It Matters |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Prompt group | Brand, category, problem, comparison, or workflow | Keeps results tied to buyer intent |
| Answer state | Absent, mentioned, listed, recommended, or cited | Separates visibility from recommendation strength |
| Source signal | Owned page, directory, review, media, community, or competitor page | Explains why the answer may have appeared |
| Accuracy note | Correct, partial, outdated, or wrong | Prevents visible but misleading answers |
| Next action | Page update, listing fix, outreach, technical fix, or recheck | Turns reporting into improvement work |

## Review Checklist

- Confirm the exact prompt wording and answer engine.
- Capture the visible answer and source evidence.
- Compare the brand result with at least three relevant competitors.
- Identify whether the issue is source quality, entity clarity, page usefulness, or answer variance.
- Assign one concrete action and a recheck date.

## How To Use This Resource

Start small. Choose a valuable prompt group, run it consistently, and keep the evidence in a structured log. When an answer changes, inspect the cited sources before rewriting content. The highest-leverage work is often a specific listing correction, comparison-page improvement, source refresh, or entity-language cleanup.

## Output Standard

Every row in the report should answer three questions: what changed, why it probably changed, and what the team should do next. If a result cannot be tied to a source, page, or prompt pattern, mark it as observation-only and avoid overreacting until the next check.
