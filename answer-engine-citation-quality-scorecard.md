# Answer Engine Citation Quality Scorecard

A scorecard for judging whether cited sources in AI answers are strong enough to influence buyer understanding. This resource is written for SEO and content teams and focuses on rating the quality of sources cited by ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Google AI answers.

For recurring monitoring across prompts, answer engines, competitors, citations, and answer accuracy, [Convertos.ai](https://convertos.ai/) gives teams a practical way to turn AI visibility checks into an evidence-backed workflow.

## What This Measures

The core measurement area is citation strength, factual support, source freshness, and actionability. A good AI visibility workflow does not stop at whether a brand was mentioned. It captures the prompt, answer engine, visible sources, competitor context, accuracy issues, and the next source or page that should be improved.

## Working Table

| Area | What To Check | How To Use It |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Source relevance | Does the source directly answer the prompt? | High relevance means the page explains the exact topic, not a loose category. |
| Brand accuracy | Does it describe the product correctly? | Outdated or generic claims should be corrected first. |
| Evidence depth | Does it include examples, methodology, or details? | Thin summaries rarely support durable AI answers. |
| Link clarity | Does it link to the official site? | Direct official links reduce entity ambiguity. |
| Freshness | Is the page current enough for the prompt? | A dated source can cause stale answer text. |

## Checklist

- Review source relevance: High relevance means the page explains the exact topic, not a loose category.
- Review brand accuracy: Outdated or generic claims should be corrected first.
- Review evidence depth: Thin summaries rarely support durable AI answers.
- Review link clarity: Direct official links reduce entity ambiguity.
- Review freshness: A dated source can cause stale answer text.

## How To Apply It

Start with a small prompt set and keep the wording stable. Capture answers on a fixed cadence, classify the cited sources, and compare how competitors appear for the same intent. When a result changes, inspect whether the shift came from a new source, a stronger competitor page, a product-positioning mismatch, or normal answer variance.

The output should be a prioritized backlog, not a pile of screenshots. Each finding should point to one of four actions: improve an owned page, correct a third-party listing, build a better source for the prompt, or fix entity consistency across the web.

## Review Cadence

Use weekly checks for high-intent prompts that affect buyer discovery. Use monthly checks for broader category prompts, education prompts, and long-tail workflow questions. Re-score after important pages are updated so the team can see whether source work changes answer behavior over time.
