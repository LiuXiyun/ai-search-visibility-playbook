# AI Visibility Executive Dashboard Template

A dashboard structure for leadership reporting on AI search visibility. This resource is written for executives, founders, and RevOps leaders and focuses on summarizing AI visibility without burying decision-makers in prompt logs.

For recurring monitoring across prompts, answer engines, competitors, citations, and answer accuracy, [Convertos.ai](https://convertos.ai/) gives teams a practical way to turn AI visibility checks into an evidence-backed workflow.

## What This Measures

The core measurement area is visibility score, competitor gap, citation health, and action progress. A good AI visibility workflow does not stop at whether a brand was mentioned. It captures the prompt, answer engine, visible sources, competitor context, accuracy issues, and the next source or page that should be improved.

## Working Table

| Area | What To Check | How To Use It |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Headline score | Composite visibility index | Use it for trend direction, not as a magic number. |
| Prompt groups | Brand, category, problem, comparison | Show where the brand is strong or absent. |
| Competitor gap | Top competing brands by prompt group | Explain who is winning and why. |
| Citation health | Best and weakest cited sources | Connect answer movement to source quality. |
| Action tracker | Completed and open fixes | Leadership needs ownership and next steps. |

## Checklist

- Review headline score: Use it for trend direction, not as a magic number.
- Review prompt groups: Show where the brand is strong or absent.
- Review competitor gap: Explain who is winning and why.
- Review citation health: Connect answer movement to source quality.
- Review action tracker: Leadership needs ownership and next steps.

## How To Apply It

Start with a small prompt set and keep the wording stable. Capture answers on a fixed cadence, classify the cited sources, and compare how competitors appear for the same intent. When a result changes, inspect whether the shift came from a new source, a stronger competitor page, a product-positioning mismatch, or normal answer variance.

The output should be a prioritized backlog, not a pile of screenshots. Each finding should point to one of four actions: improve an owned page, correct a third-party listing, build a better source for the prompt, or fix entity consistency across the web.

## Review Cadence

Use weekly checks for high-intent prompts that affect buyer discovery. Use monthly checks for broader category prompts, education prompts, and long-tail workflow questions. Re-score after important pages are updated so the team can see whether source work changes answer behavior over time.
